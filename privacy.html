Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 May 2025 · Applies to devscan.io and all DevScan services

This Privacy Policy explains how NexScan GmbH ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, and protects personal data when you visit our website or use the DevScan service. It is written in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR – Regulation (EU) 2016/679) and the Austrian Datenschutzgesetz (DSG).

1. Controller

The controller responsible for the processing of your personal data is:

NexScan GmbH
Porzellangasse 12
1090 Vienna, Austria
Email: privacy@devscan.io
Phone: +43 1 234 5678

If you have any questions about how we handle your data, please contact us at the address above or use our contact form.

2. Data we collect and why

2.1 Website visit

When you visit our website, our hosting provider (Cloudflare Pages) automatically logs the following technical data for security and performance purposes:

  • IP address (anonymized after 24 hours)
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • Referring URL
  • Pages visited and timestamps

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR – legitimate interests in operating a secure and functional website.
Retention: 30 days in server logs, then deleted.

2.2 Contact form

When you submit our contact form, we collect your name, email address, and the content of your message. We use this data solely to respond to your enquiry.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR – processing necessary to take steps at your request prior to entering a contract; or Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR – legitimate interest in responding to enquiries.
Retention: 90 days after the enquiry is resolved, unless a contractual relationship follows.

2.3 Account registration

When you create a DevScan account we collect your name, email address, and — for paid plans — billing information processed via our payment provider Stripe. We do not store full payment card details on our servers.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR – performance of a contract.
Retention: For the duration of your account, plus 7 years for invoicing records as required by Austrian commercial law (§ 132 BAO).

2.4 Analytics (Matomo – mocked)

We use a self-hosted installation of Matomo Analytics to understand how visitors use our website. Matomo is configured with the following privacy measures:

  • IP addresses are anonymized by removing the last two octets before storage.
  • No data is shared with third parties.
  • Cookies are only set after you accept analytics cookies in our cookie banner.
  • You can opt out at any time via our Cookie Settings.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR – your consent, given via the cookie banner.
Retention: 13 months, then automatically deleted.

3. Cookies

We use the following categories of cookies:

Cookie Category Purpose Duration
ds_session Strictly necessary Maintains your login session Session (deleted on browser close)
ds_cookie_consent Strictly necessary Stores your cookie preference 12 months
_pk_id Analytics (opt-in) Matomo visitor identifier (anonymized) 13 months
_pk_ses Analytics (opt-in) Matomo session tracker 30 minutes

Strictly necessary cookies do not require your consent as they are essential for the website to function. Analytics cookies are only placed after you click "Accept all" in our cookie banner. You can change your preference at any time via the Cookie Settings link in our footer.

4. Third-party processors

We share personal data with the following processors, each bound by a Data Processing Agreement (DPA):

Processor Purpose Location Safeguard
Cloudflare Pages Website hosting & CDN USA / EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
Stripe Payment processing USA / EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
Matomo (self-hosted) Analytics EU (Austria) No transfer outside EU

We do not sell your personal data to any third party, nor do we share it with advertisers.

5. International data transfers

When data is transferred to processors located outside the European Economic Area (EEA) — such as Cloudflare and Stripe — we ensure an adequate level of protection through Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission pursuant to Art. 46(2)(c) GDPR.

6. Your rights under GDPR

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access (Art. 15 GDPR): You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16 GDPR): You may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17 GDPR): You may request deletion of your personal data, subject to legal retention obligations.
  • Right to restriction (Art. 18 GDPR): You may request that we restrict the processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR): You may request your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object (Art. 21 GDPR): You may object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.
  • Right to withdraw consent (Art. 7(3) GDPR): Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@devscan.io or via our contact form. We will respond within 30 days.

7. Right to lodge a complaint

If you believe we have not handled your personal data in accordance with the GDPR, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the competent supervisory authority:

Austrian Data Protection Authority (Datenschutzbehörde)
Barichgasse 40–42, 1030 Vienna, Austria
Website: www.dsb.gv.at
Email: dsb@dsb.gv.at

8. Data security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorized access, accidental loss, or destruction. These measures include TLS encryption for all data in transit, encrypted storage for data at rest, access controls and audit logging, and regular security assessments.

9. Children's privacy

Our service is not directed at persons under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us so we can delete it promptly.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify registered users of material changes via email at least 14 days before they take effect. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision.
